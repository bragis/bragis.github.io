My name is Bragi Sveinsson, I develop Magnetic Resonance Imaging technology.
I have the pleasure of working as a Research Fellow in the Low-Field Imaging Laboratory, directed by Matthew Rosen at the Athinoula A. Martinos Center for Biomedical Imaging at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School. I am also an associate of the Harvard University Department of Physics.

Before, I worked as a postdoctoral fellow in the Body Magnetic Resonance (BMR) group, directed by Brian Hargreaves, within the Radiological Sciences Laboratory (RSL) at Stanford University.

My main research interests are MRI at low fields, quantitative steady state imaging of cartilage for early detection of osteoarthritis and MRI near metallic implants.
